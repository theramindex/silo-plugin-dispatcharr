# Dispatcharr Design Context

## Users

Self-hosted Silo viewers use Dispatcharr to scan live television, find sports coverage quickly, and move between live broadcasts and matched replays. They often use the interface from a couch, tablet, or desktop and need clear status, large targets, and predictable back navigation.

## Brand And Tone

Dispatcharr should feel like a native part of Silo: calm, cinematic, precise, and media-forward without becoming a separate streaming brand. Operational screens stay compact and legible; viewing surfaces can use real program, league, team, and replay artwork when it is available.

## Visual Direction

- Dark Silo surfaces with restrained borders and no decorative gradients or fake atmospheric artwork.
- Real media is the strongest visual signal. When artwork is unavailable, use team logos, league labels, and disciplined typography.
- Use cards only for individual events, leagues, teams, broadcasts, and replay items.
- Keep corner radii at 8px or below and controls at least 44px on touch layouts.
- Prefer dense, scannable rails and grids over oversized empty hero treatments.

## Sports Principles

1. Live status and the primary playable broadcast are immediately obvious.
2. A sports event is one identity that joins scores, Dispatcharr channels, and high-confidence Silo replay matches.
3. Navigation follows Sports browse to league to event detail, with an explicit back path at every level.
4. Missing metadata must degrade honestly; never invent artwork, sessions, scores, or replay coverage.
5. Desktop, tablet, and mobile preserve the same information hierarchy with no hover-only actions.
6. Sports-first playback is scoped to launches from Sports and does not change normal Live TV playback.
